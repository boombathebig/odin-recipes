Fried Chicken

Description

Buttery, flakey, salty yet sweet... those are descriptors for the perfect fried chicken recipe. Welcome to heaven.

After embarking on this taste journey, you might as well just cancel your subscription to the KFC family pass because this fowl is gonna rock your world.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • kosher salt
  • 4 pounds chicken (rinsed and cut into 10 pieces)
  • vegetable oil (or shortening)
  • 1 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ground black pepper (freshly)

Steps

  1. Pour 4 cups cold water into a large bowl. Add sugar and I cup of salt and stir until dissolved. Place chicken in brine and set aside in a cool place to soak for 1 –2 hours. Drain, rinse, then pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Pour oil into a large cast-iron skillet to a depth of 2″, then heat over medium heat to 350° on a candy thermometer. Meanwhile, sift together flour, baking powder, 1⁄2 tsp. salt, and H tsp. pepper in a large plastic bag. Place chicken in bag one piece at a time and shake in seasoned flour until well coated.
  3. Fry chicken in hot oil, larger pieces first, skin side down (working in batches if skillet won’t hold all the pieces at the same time), turning once, until golden and crispy, about 8 minutes per side. Transfer chicken to a wire rack set over paper towels to drain, and season to taste with salt and pepper.
